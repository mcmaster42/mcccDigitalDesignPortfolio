The Fun Stuff

My Far Side Inspiration

Gary Larson's "The Far Side" has been a massive influence on my artistic style and sense of humor. First published in 1979, Larson's single-panel comics combined absurdist humor with animals, humans, and science in unexpected ways.

What I admire most about Larson's work is how he uses simple drawings to convey complex jokes, often playing with perspective and the unexpected. His anthropomorphic animals, clueless humans, and bizarre scenarios taught me that humor can be found by simply viewing the ordinary world from a slightly different angle.

Fun Facts About Me

Odd Collections

I collect unusual salt and pepper shakers, particularly ones shaped like animals in human scenarios.

Hidden Talent

I can identify most insects by their scientific names - a skill that frequently inspires my artwork.

My Workspace

My drawing desk is surrounded by obscure science textbooks, animal figurines, and at least one cup of forgotten coffee.

Daily Ritual

I start each morning by drawing the weirdest thing I can imagine in under five minutes. It keeps my creativity flowing!
